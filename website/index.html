Climate change's impact on water in California

Californians' water usage increases with each drought

By Teresa Liu

California has had many severe droughts throughout its history. The dry weather significantly depletes water resources and creates even longer period of dry spells. Water scarcity could lead to a series of problems, from fallowed farmlands to water pollution.

The current drought began in late 2020. As of July 2022, almost 60 percent of the state is in “extreme drought” or “exceptional drought”, according to the US Drought Monitor. It is likely to surpass the 2012-2016 drought to become the worst drought in the state’s history.

The drought and the ensuing water restrictions sparked my interest in this project. When analyzing data from The California Water Boards, I noticed that water production/consumption jumps during warmer months or whenever the state is in drought. For example, the following chart shows that water production is the highest in 2014, a year that was ranked by NASA as the warmest on record since 1880. The year 2021 followed closely behind. Even though we are only half way through 2022, the chart shows that the water production for this year is already pretty close to the entire year's water production of 2019.

California is divided into 10 hydrologic regions by the state water boards. If we look at each region by itself, as in the second chart, we notice that the highest/lowest water production dates of each area correspond to the weather at the time. For example, for South Coast, which covers cities such as Los Angeles, San Diego and Santa Barbara, water volume peaks in July 2014, one of the hottest months of the last drought. Water production of South Coast is the lowest in Feb, 2019, which was a winter month and a drought-free period in the state.

I set out to compare water usage with water production, but based on my analysis in Panda, water usage has a positive correlation with water production, which make me comes to the conclusion that water production and usage both increase with warmer temperature. For a future project, I would like to know how effective mandatory water restrictions are in curbing water usage.
